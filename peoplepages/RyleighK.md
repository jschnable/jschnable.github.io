Ryleigh J. Kirby is a Ph.D. student at the University of Nebraska–Lincoln. Since joining the Schnable Lab in 2022 as an undergraduate researcher, she has contributed to multiple projects at the intersection of plant genetics, comparative genomics, and high‑throughput phenotyping. Ryleigh has co‑authored peer‑reviewed publications in The Plant Phenome Journal and PeerJ, and her work has been presented at national conferences including the Maize Genetics Meeting and the National Corn Congress. She further gained industry experience as a North American Discovery Breeding Intern with Syngenta in 2023, where she collaborated with breeders and senior scientists on pipeline optimization.
In addition to her research, Ryleigh serves as a teaching assistant for both introductory and advanced plant science courses. She is President of the UNL Plant Biology Club and a member of the CASNR Student Advisory Board. Ryleigh is passionate about applying computational and genomic approaches to support breeding programs, and as a first‑generation researcher, she is driven by the goal of improving crop systems through science.
